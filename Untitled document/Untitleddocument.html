Lia Call is a 21-year-old artist and trans woman with a sound and new media practice currently operating in Chicago.

Call downloads open-source sound samples or 3D objects found on the internet and combines them with objects recorded or scanned from the physical world. She merges the broken image of a 3D scan or poorly modeled object into one chimera. A jank Frankenstein trying to be stunning. Removing hierarchical relationships between both physical and virtual objects allows one to open up a richer critique of some human biases as well as feel the value of the virtual. 3D scanning her own body is like the taxidermy of an animal, but instead of capturing an external piece of nature and conquering it, it is recontextualizing a piece of nature that the artist already possesses. Within a physical context, the authenticity of the artist’s body is contested. Her biological womanhood is contingent on contemporary western medical technologies. This fact is weaponized against trans women, turning their cyborgness against them without considering the dread of this existing under these contingencies. Within a virtual context, the artist has complete control of the scan of her body, taking back an autonomy she has been denied elsewhere.
